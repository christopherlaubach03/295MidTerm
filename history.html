Detroit Pistons History

The Detroit Pistons are a professional basketball team based in Detroit, Michigan, with a rich history that dates back to 1941. Founded by Fred Zollner originally known as the Fort Wayne Pistons, due the the team playing in Fort Wayne, Indiana, and a part of the Basketball Association of America (BAA). Later BAA merged with the National Basketball League (NBL) to form the NBA in 1949. The Pistons moved to Detroit in 1957 gaining much attention. They achieved their greatest success in the 1980s and 1990s by winning back-to-back NBA championships led by stars like Isiah Thomas, Joe Dumars, and Bill Laimbeer. After a period of regrouping the Pistons claimed their third NBA title in 2004, with a team lead by Chauncey Billups, Ben Wallace, and Richard Hamilton. The Pistons remain one of the NBA’s most iconic franchises known for their grit, defense, and passionate fans.
